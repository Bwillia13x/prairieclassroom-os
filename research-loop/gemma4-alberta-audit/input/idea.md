# Idea

Audit of PrairieClassroom OS novelty, Gemma 4 application, and Alberta classroom-complexity viability

## Core Question

- How novel is PrairieClassroom OS's actual use of Gemma 4 relative to mainstream teacher-AI products and to current Gemma capabilities?
- Could this platform credibly demonstrate a game-changing but realistic contribution to Alberta education, especially for classroom-complexity challenges, without overclaiming what software can solve?

## Subquestions

- Which product claims are already evidenced in the repository versus still aspirational?
- Which aspects are genuinely Gemma-native, and which are generic LLM application patterns?
- How well does the product align with Alberta's current classroom-complexity, multilingual, collaboration, and privacy context?
- What is differentiated about the adult-coordination and classroom-memory thesis compared with existing teacher AI platforms?
- What evidence would be needed to justify a stronger "game-changing" claim to educators, school leaders, or provincial stakeholders?

## Jurisdictions

- Alberta, with Calgary-specific evidence where provincial evidence is thin
- Comparative market context: North American teacher-AI product landscape

## Stakeholders

- Classroom teachers in Alberta K-6 settings
- Educational assistants and complexity teams
- School and division leaders responsible for implementation and privacy
- Alberta Education and Childcare policy and program stakeholders
- Families affected by communication quality and timing
