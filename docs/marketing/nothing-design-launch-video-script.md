## Creative Direction

This cut should feel like an instrument panel in a dark classroom before the bell: OLED black, white data, one signal color at a time, hard edges, no decorative glow, no generic gradients, no card-heavy SaaS gloss. The design language is the app's Nothing-inspired system: monochrome industrial UI, mono labels, segmented progress, flat chrome, and restrained red/green/amber status.

The story is not "AI makes school easy." The story is "coordination load becomes visible enough for adults to act."

## Voiceover Script

PrairieClassroom OS starts where complex classrooms actually start: before the bell, with too many open threads.

This is not a student chatbot. It is a command surface for the adults carrying the room.

The Today view makes the day visible. Open follow-ups. Students to check first. Patterns that should not stay hidden. A fragile block that needs preparation before it becomes urgent.

The product calls this complexity debt. Not to blame the teacher, and not to score children, but to expose the coordination work that compounds when nobody can see it.

From one lesson artifact, the system builds several access routes through the same learning goal. Core path. Language support. Chunked scaffold. Extension. EA small group. The expectation stays intact; the route adapts to the learners in the room.

Family communication stays bounded. The system can help draft clear messages, but nothing leaves automatically. The teacher reviews, edits, and chooses the school channel. The copilot drafts. The teacher decides.

For educational assistants, PrairieClassroom turns classroom memory into a route through the day: watch points, time blocks, pre-corrections, and handoffs that can be shared before the hallway briefing is already too late.

The forecast moves planning upstream. It does not predict children. It surfaces support pressure from observed classroom patterns, so the adult team can prepare while there is still time.

The evidence loop stays local: feedback, sessions, generated artifacts, and reliability signals for pilot review without turning the classroom into an analytics warehouse.

PrairieClassroom OS is local-first, teacher-controlled, and bounded by design. No diagnosis. No surveillance. No autonomous sends.

Less invisible coordination drag for teachers. More timely support for students. A classroom operating layer for the adults holding the room together.

## NotebookLM Context

If this script is uploaded to NotebookLM, treat the Remotion render as a design reference and this document as the product truth source. The cinematic version should preserve these claims:

- PrairieClassroom OS is a local-first classroom complexity copilot for Alberta K-6 teachers and EAs.
- It is not a student chatbot, diagnostic tool, automated discipline engine, or surveillance dashboard.
- It helps adults coordinate differentiated materials, family communication, EA support, forecasts, and evidence loops.
- It uses Gemma 4 model routing in the broader product architecture, with fast live workflows and deeper planning workflows.
- Family messages are drafts only; teachers approve anything outward-facing.
- Forecasting means support-complexity forecasting, not predicting individual student behavior.
- The intended production posture is privacy-preserving and classroom-scoped.

Avoid claims that the product has been deployed with real student data, diagnoses learners, replaces teachers, or sends anything autonomously.

## Visual Notes For Editors

- Keep the first frame dark, quiet, and sparse.
- Use hard cuts or short fades, never bouncy motion.
- Pair every UI shot with one short claim.
- Use one signal color at a time: white for structure, amber for attention, green for confirmed output, red for safety boundaries or pressure.
- When adding music, choose a low pulse rather than triumphant startup music.
- If adding live-action footage, use practical classroom objects: lesson pages, printed schedules, a quiet laptop, a teacher's notebook, an EA schedule. Avoid holograms and generic AI visuals.
